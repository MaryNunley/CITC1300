About the Author:

Pre-Author Days

Kim Harrison, whose pen name is Dawn Cook, was born on July 28, 1966, and is a well-known author in the genre of urban fantasy. Before becoming a full-time writer, Harrison had a diverse career, including stints in advertising and freelance writing. Her varied professional background has helped shape the dynamic and layered narratives she is known for today. Her early works, which included short stories, gave her the foundation to write her breakthrough novel Dead Witch Walking in 2004. This first book in The Hollows series quickly captured the attention of readers, establishing Harrison as a key voice in urban fantasy literature.

Harrisons Personal Life

Despite her public success, Harrison maintains a relatively low profile in her personal life. She keeps much of her private life out of the spotlight and prefers to let her writing speak for itself. This modest approach to fame has allowed her to focus on her craft and maintain a connection with her readers without the distractions of public scrutiny. Many fans appreciate her humility and the authenticity she brings to both her books and her interactions with readers.

Other Genres

In addition to The Hollows, Harrison has written other successful works, including The Madison Avery series and various short stories. Her writing is known for its balance of action, romance, and humor, with strong female protagonists at the forefront. Although her works are predominantly set in fantastical, supernatural worlds, they often deal with universal themes such as self-identity, moral dilemmas, and the consequences of one’s actions. These relatable themes have contributed to Harrison’s widespread appeal across different age groups.
